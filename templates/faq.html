Hello, how can we help you?

General

I do not have any prior knowledge of git and/or   any programming language. Can I do this?
Absolutely. This program specially is made for people like you who want to learn. We,the mentors, will help you wherever possible.

Where does KWoC occur? Do I need to stay in Kharagpur?
Kharagpur Winter of Code occurs entirely online , there is no requirement of staying back in the campus.

What is the purpose of this program? Is this some kind of internship?
No, this is just a program which helps the students to learn how to contribute in the open-source world. This activity prepares you for programs like Google Summer of Code.

Can I participate in KWoC as both a mentor and a student?
A student can work on any project as long as his work is evaluated/ reviewed by a KWoC mentor.

What can I do to help spread the word about KWoC?
Please visit our facebook page : KOSS and share our posts. You can also share the link of our website : KWoC. We would be very happy if you do that.

What if I have a question not answered in this FAQ?
Please make sure to read the website. If after reading through it you still don't have an answer please consider reaching out to our page.

What are the start and end dates of the programme?
Check the timeline

Students

Can I show the contributions that I have made in KWoC for participating in any competitions?
All the contributions that you will make in KWoC will be displayed on your GitHub timeline. So, that is a big plus if you are participating in any competitions and your github profile is on display.

Can I apply if I am not a student of IIT Kharagpur?
If you are not a student of IIT Kharagpur and yet have come to know about KWoC, then sure you can apply ! Although our aim of the prgram is to close monitor the students of the campus.

Is there any eligibility criteria?
There is no strict eligibility criteria as such. At the end of the day, you need to be a beginner at Open Source contributions and the will to learn.

When should I apply?
The last date of student registration is 14th December. That means you can apply anytime before that. But to take the maximum out of it, apply before 1st December.

Can you provide me any advice so that I can start preparing for it beforehand?
Play around git and github. Create an account on GitHub, set up git on your machine and start experimenting with it.

What are my chances of receiving a position in KWoC?
Pretty fair ! You need to be working for the whole month regardless of your expertise level.

Are their any other similar kind of programme that I might find of interest?
If you mean similar to KWoC, then this is the second time anything like this is happening in KGP. If you mean similar to GSoC, then look around, there are plenty of them e.g. KDE summer of code, Outreachy, Rails summer of code, etc.

How much time in a day do I need to give for KWoC?
GSoC requires 40 hours per week in summers. For KWoC, you may give 1-2 hours daily and that includes conversation with your mentor(s). More the project you choose, more time in conversing about the work.

Will I receive a certificate at the end of the KWoC 2016?
If you remain with us till the end of the program, then you'll most likely receive a certificate for completion of the prgram.

Which programming language(s) do I need to be fluent in?
Sky is the limit. You need to see the programming languages of the listed projects. But we recommend you one or more than one of Python, C++, JAVA and Javascript.

Will taking part in KWoC help me in being a part of KOSS?
Of course yes! :)

Whom should I contact or clear my doubts if my mentor is temporarily unavailable?
Contact us ASAP. Write to kossiitkgp@gmail.com.

Can I work on more than one project?
Yes sure! You can even work parallely on them.

Are there any deadlines during the session? Are those deadlines strict? What if I am unable to meet them due to some unavoidable reasons?
See the timeline for deadlines. In any case, make sure you keep us informed.

Will this programme help me in improving competitive coding skills or get selected for ACM ICPC?
This question is very debatable. Participating in Open Source contribution will improve your skills on software development and of a particular programming language. Depending on your project, you might be able to use some of the algorithms too. But direct implications on ICPC will not be very helpful.

What are the minimum contributions I   have to do to get successfully recognized as to have completed the programme?
You need to have at least 3 Pull requests merged by the end of the program.
